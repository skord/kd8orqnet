This hands-on guide demonstrates how to use the Erlang programming language and its OTP framework of reusable libraries, tools, and design principles to develop complex commercial-grade systems that simply cannot fail.\n\nIn the first part of the book, you’ll learn how to design and implement process behaviors and supervision trees with Erlang/OTP, and bundle them into standalone nodes. The second part addresses reliability, scalability, and high availability in your overall system design. If you’re familiar with Erlang, this book will help you understand the design choices and trade-offs necessary to keep your system running.\n\n \n Explore OTP’s building blocks: the Erlang language, tools and libraries collection, and its abstract principles and design rules \n Dive into the fundamentals of OTP reusable frameworks: the Erlang process structures OTP uses for behaviors \n Understand how OTP behaviors support client-server structures, finite state machine patterns, event handling, and runtime/code integration \n Write your own behaviors and special processes \n Use OTP’s tools, techniques, and architectures to handle deployment, monitoring, and operations"
