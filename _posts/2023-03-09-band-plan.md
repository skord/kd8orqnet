Significant regional variations in both current band utilization and the intensity and frequency distribution of noise sources preclude one plan that is suitable for all parts of the country.  These variations will require many regional frequency coordinators to maintain band plans that differ in some respects from any national plan.  As with all band plans, locally coordinated plans always take precedence over any general recommendations such as a national band plan.

[^3]: May be used for either repeater inputs or weak-signal as regional needs dictate

[^5]: These segments may also be designated regionally to accommodate alternative repeater splits.
[^6]: Simplex FM calling frequency 927.500 or regionally selected alternative.
[^7]: Additional FM simplex frequencies may be designated regionally.

### Above 10.50 GHz*

All modes and licensees (except Novices) are authorized Amateur Bands above 10.5 GHz.

US amateurs must check Sections 97.301, 97.303, 97.305 and 97.307 for sharing requirements before operating.
